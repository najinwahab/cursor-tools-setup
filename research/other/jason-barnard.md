# The AI Engine Pipeline: 10 Gates That Decide Whether You Win the Recommendation

**Author**: Jason Barnard

**Document Type**: Long-Form Technical Article / Framework

**Source**: Search Engine Land

**URL**: https://searchengineland.com/ai-engine-pipeline-gates-470610

**Date**: March 3, 2026

## Summary

Jason Barnard introduces his DSCRI-ARGDW framework — a 10-gate pipeline that
every piece of content must pass before it becomes an AI recommendation.
The gates are: Discovered, Selected, Crawled, Rendered, Indexed, Annotated,
Recruited, Grounded, Displayed, and Won — organized into three acts serving
three audiences: bots, algorithms, and people respectively.

The article's core insight is that cascading confidence is multiplicative, not
additive — a single weak gate destroys your entire pipeline signal, similar to
Darwinian natural selection. Barnard argues that most brands are losing at the
Annotation gate (Gate 6) — the most underaddressed phase — where AI decides
what your content means, which entity it belongs to, and whether it is worth
recruiting into search, knowledge graphs, or LLM training data.
