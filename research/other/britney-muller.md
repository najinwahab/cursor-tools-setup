# The Future of AI in Search | Whiteboard Friday Revisited With Britney Muller

**Author**:  Jo Cameron, **Edited by**: Meghan Pahinui

**Document Type**: Written Article / Video Companion

**Source**: Moz Blog

**URL**: https://moz.com/blog/the-future-of-ai-in-search-whiteboard-friday-revisited-with-britney-muller

**Date**: September 19, 2025

## Summary

This article reflects on a 2020 presentation by Britney Muller, examining how the landscape of AI in search has evolved from initial skepticism to widespread adoption of Large Language Models (LLMs). It addresses the challenge marketers face in navigating the current "AI hype," offering practical advice on how to use AI efficiently without losing the essential human element in their work. 

To successfully navigate this landscape, the article emphasizes that the enduring value of content lies in human expertise, as AI cannot replicate real-world experiences, unique voices, or personal anecdotes. Furthermore, it suggests that marketers should shift their focus away from using generative AI for content creation—which Muller calls one of its "worst capabilities"—and instead leverage it for data analysis, automating mundane tasks, and building simple tools.

# Actionable AI for Marketers — The Human in the Loop

**Author**: William Harris

**Document Type**: Written Interview / Article

**Source**: Elumynt

**URL**: https://www.elumynt.com/podcast/actionable-ai-for-marketers-the-human-in-the-loop-with-britney-muller

**Date**: April 1, 2025

## Summary

This podcast interview features AI consultant Britney Muller discussing how marketers can move beyond the "hype" to leverage AI for intentional automation and actionable insights rather than relying on it to mass-produce generic content. It addresses the misconception that AI should be used as a primary research engine or fact-checker, emphasizing instead its true utility in processing large datasets, identifying patterns, and streamlining workflows without sacrificing brand authenticity. 

For AI-powered SEO content production, a crucial insight is that "brand mentions are the new backlinks," meaning marketers must find creative ways to get their products naturally referenced online, as Large Language Models use these semantic votes of confidence to shape their outputs. Furthermore, the episode highlights the value of using AI to analyze real-time consumer sentiment—such as scraping Reddit APIs to uncover genuine audience pain points—and transforming website content into vector embeddings to identify content gaps and improve internal linking architecture.
