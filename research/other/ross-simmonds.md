# Ross Simmonds - Content Distribution in The Age of AI (GURU)

**Author**: Ross Simmonds

**Document Type**: Conference Presentation / Written Summary

**Source**: Scribd (GURU Conference)

**URL**: https://www.scribd.com/document/991029399/Ross-Simmonds-Content-Distribution-in-the-Age-of-AI-GURU

**Date**: October 2025

## Summary

Ross Simmonds presents his Content Growth Framework for the AI era,
structured around four pillars: Research, Creation, Distribution, and
Optimization. The central argument is that most content fails not because it
is poorly written but because it is never strategically distributed — and AI
now makes it possible to automate the distribution layer at scale without
sacrificing quality or brand voice.

Key insight: Ross introduces the concept of a "distribution flywheel" where a
single long-form asset — a podcast episode, blog post, or research report —
is automatically repurposed across email, LinkedIn, and social channels using
AI tools. This fundamentally changes the economics of content marketing,
making small teams capable of competing with large editorial operations.

from manual amplification to fully automated distribution engines.

# Create Once, Distrubute Forever

**Author**: Ross Simmonds

**Document Type**: Popular Business/Self-help Book/Free Chapter

**Source**: rosssimmonds.com

**URL**: https://rosssimmonds.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Create-Once-Distribute-Forever-By-Ross-Simmonds-Free-Chapter.pdf

**Date**: March, 2024

## Summary

Ross Simmonds's book argues that the single biggest mistake content creators and businesses make is spending all their time making content and almost no time spreading it. His core argument is simple: it is not the best content that wins, it is the best distribution that wins. He uses real examples — a blog post about coffee and hip-hop that went from zero views to 35,000 in one day after he shared it across Reddit, LinkedIn, and Facebook groups; a SaaS article that brought in a $250,000 client — to show that the same piece of content, run through a proper distribution strategy, can generate life-changing revenue. His formula is: create once, distribute forever — meaning you publish something once but keep actively spreading it across multiple platforms and communities for as long as it remains relevant.

This particular free chapter does not go into specific detail about using AI for content creation or distribution — that topic is not covered in these opening chapters. What it does say about technology generally is that modern tools have made distribution easier and cheaper than ever before. Anyone with a phone and internet access can now reach the same size audience that once required a million-dollar media budget. It also notes that the number of marketing technology companies has grown from 150 in 2011 to over 9,500 today, showing how rapidly the tools available to content creators have expanded. The book's structure suggests that AI and specific platform strategies are covered in later chapters — particularly Part III (the channel-by-channel guide) and Part IV (The Content Distribution Playbook, page 257) — which are not included in this free chapter sample.
