# SEOFOMO’s Organic Traditional & AI Search Trends for 2026: Top Challenges, Expectations & Actions

**Author**: Aleyda Solis

**Document Type**: Industry Survey / Research Report

**Source**: SEOFOMO Hub

**URL**: https://hub.seofomo.co/surveys/organic-search-trends/

**Date**: Dec 27, 2026

## Summary

This article outlines the findings of SEOFOMO’s 2026 Organic Search Trends Survey, detailing how experienced professionals are navigating the evolving landscape of traditional and AI-driven search. It primarily addresses the industry's struggle with declining click-through rates and attribution challenges by highlighting the necessary shift from traditional traffic metrics to tracking brand influence and visibility. 

Major insight for AI-powered content production is that AI models rely heavily on structured, citation-ready content that prioritizes clear expertise and original data over mass-produced volume. Consequently, successful SEO strategies must move away from simple keyword targeting in favor of building strong, authoritative brand entities across multiple platforms to ensure they are consistently referenced by AI engines.

# The State of Ecommerce SEO & AI Search in 2026

**Author**: Aleyda Solis

**Document Type**: Conference Presentation / Slide Deck

**Source**: Speaker Deck (SMX Munich 2026)

**URL**: https://speakerdeck.com/aleyda/the-state-of-ecommerce-seo-and-ai-search-in-2026-what-are-the-shifts-and-the-top-actions-to-take-

**Date**: March 9, 2026

## Summary

This presentation addresses the critical challenge of online retailers losing organic traffic as AI-driven search models increasingly provide direct answers to consumers. To survive this shift, ecommerce businesses must pivot from traditional traffic metrics to optimizing for overall brand visibility and authority. 

Succeeding in this new visibility-focused landscape requires content creators to build comprehensive topical authority rather than just targeting isolated keywords. Additionally, this content must be formatted into easily extractable modules—using clear lists, tables, and concise facts—so AI models can reliably synthesize and cite the brand in their product recommendations.
