# A Reflection on SEO, GEO & AI Search in 2025

**Author**: Lily Ray

**Document Type**: Personal Essay / Year-in-Review Analysis

**Source**: Substack (Lily Ray Newsletter)

**URL**: https://substack.com/home/post/p-184264905

**Date**: Jan 2026

## Summary

Lily Ray reflects on how search transformed dramatically in 2025 — covering
the rise of AI Overviews, the blurring line between GEO and traditional SEO,
and her observations on which content types survived algorithm volatility.
Written in her signature honest, data-backed tone, this piece captures the
pivotal turning point when AI search stopped being theoretical and became
operational for practitioners.

Key insight: Lily argues that the brands that retained and grew traffic in
2025 were not those who chased AI optimization tactics, but those who had
already invested in genuine E-E-A-T signals, brand authority, and content
depth — a message that runs counter to most industry hype.

## Summary

Lily Ray provides a complete breakdown of the state of search and AI in 2026,
specifically targeting publishers, affiliate marketers, and brands navigating
Google's increasingly aggressive core updates. She outlines why small and
medium businesses are being hit hardest and offers a strategic roadmap
covering content adjustments, off-site brand mentions, and surviving the shift
to AI-driven search.

The piece is particularly valuable for its focus on Google's 2026 crackdown
dynamics — Lily explains how AI Overviews and quality thresholds are
compounding each other, creating a two-tier web where brand-backed content
dominates and anonymous or thin content disappears entirely.
