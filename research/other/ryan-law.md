# AI Content Wasn't Good Enough. Now It Is.

**Author**: Ryan Law

**Document Type**: Research-Backed Blog Article

**Source**: Ahrefs Blog

**URL**: https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-content-wasnt-good-enough-now-it-is/

**Date**: March 16, 2026

## Summary
Ryan Law makes a bold evidence-based argument: AI-generated content has
crossed the quality threshold and is now competitive with human writing for
most content types — but only when built on the right structure. He outlines
the exact criteria that make AI content succeed: addressing primary search
intent, building on existing consensus, filling topic gaps, and adding novel
information above what competitors have published.

The piece is particularly important because it comes from Ahrefs — one of
the most data-rich SEO platforms — lending it empirical credibility. Ryan's
framework for evaluating AI content quality (not by origin but by outcome)
is one of the clearest and most actionable guides for AI-powered SEO content
production available in 2026.

# Website traffic decline in 2026: Ryan Law on AI overviews, zero-click search and what to do next

**Author**: Ryan Law

**Document Type**: Conference Talk Summary / Written Report

**Source**: The Marketing Meetup

**URL**: https://themarketingmeetup.com/events/website-traffic-decline-2026/

**Date**: February 26, 2026

## Summary
Ryan Law shares Ahrefs research showing that AI Overviews now appear on
roughly 20% of all queries, and when they do, the #1 ranking result loses
approximately 50% of its click-through rate. He argues this makes traditional
traffic volume a misleading success metric and introduces Ahrefs' "business
potential" scoring system — rating every content topic from 0 to 3 based on
how naturally the product can be mentioned.

The key framework shift: stop optimizing for traffic, start optimizing for
topical relevance to your product. This directly ties content strategy to
revenue outcomes and provides a practical model for deciding which AI search
visibility battles are actually worth fighting.
